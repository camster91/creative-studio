# Photoroom vs Photogen

If you're running a CPG or DTC brand and you ship a lot of product photos, you've probably heard of **Photoroom**. They've been the go-to "remove the background, put a clean backdrop" tool since before AI image generation was a thing. **Photogen** is the tool we built for the same customer — solo CPG marketers who need campaign-ready creative, not just a background-removed PNG.

This isn't a hit piece on Photoroom. It's a straight comparison so you can pick the right tool for the job.

## What Photoroom does well

- **Background removal** is fast, accurate, and battle-tested across millions of products.
- **Templates and presets** for Amazon, Shopify, Instagram, etc. Photoroom has more templates than we do (years head start).
- **Brand kits and team features** — if you have 5 people all using the same logo, fonts, and color palette, Photoroom has the workflow nailed.
- **Onboarding is fast** — drag a photo, get a clean PNG in 30 seconds.

## What Photogen does well

- **Scene generation, not just background removal.** Photogen's headline feature is *scene-set*: drop a product photo, get five different lifestyle scenes (in-hand, studio, action, lifestyle, with-props) in one click. Photoroom will give you a clean cutout. Photogen will give you five campaign-ready shots.
- **Real product compositing** — the AI builds the scene *around* your product, not *instead of* it. Your flavor name, label text, and packaging stay intact. Midjourney hallucinates fake SKUs; Photogen doesn't.
- **Locked aspect ratios for real ad placements** — 1:1 for Amazon, 4:5 for Instagram feed, 9:16 for Pinterest, 16:9 for email banners. Every generation is already the right shape.
- **Brings its own API key** — costs go directly to your Google account. Photoroom charges a subscription on top of any AI cost.

## What both tools get wrong

- **No "I want this exact color, but slightly warmer"** control. Generative AI doesn't expose granular creative controls the way Photoshop does. Both Photoroom and Photogen trade some control for speed.
- **Pricing transparency.** Photoroom's AI tier is layered behind a subscription. Photogen is BYOK + free trial + paid plans, which is more transparent but means you need a Google account.
- **Hair, fur, and translucent packaging.** Both tools struggle with these edge cases. Photoroom has slightly better fine-edge handling because its background-removal model is more mature.

## The short version

If you mostly need **clean cutouts and product-page PNGs**, Photoroom is great. If you need **lifestyle scenes and ad creative at scale**, Photogen is the better fit. A lot of brands we talk to use both — Photoroom for the product page, Photogen for the launch campaign.
